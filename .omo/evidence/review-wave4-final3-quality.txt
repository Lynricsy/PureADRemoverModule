## blocking issues

### 1. `profile-restore` folds distinct component records across Android users

Severity: high.

`crates/puread-cli/src/profile_execute/profile_restore/final_state.rs:75-96` builds `ComponentActionKey` from only:

- `rule_id`
- `package`
- `component`

It omits `user_id`, even though component apply/restore is explicitly user-scoped:

- `crates/puread-android/src/profiles/executor/appops_components.rs:82` applies `pm disable-user --user <user_id>`.
- `crates/puread-android/src/profiles/executor/appops_components.rs:101` restores `pm enable --user <user_id>`.

Because `component_target_position` and `restored_indexes_for` both use this incomplete key (`final_state.rs:48-72`), two durable component records with the same rule/package/component but different `user_id` are treated as one logical restore action. The targeted CLI reproduction proved the real surface impact:

- pending restore count became 1 for 2 records.
- only `/system/bin/pm enable --user 10 ...` ran.
- both `user_id=0` and `user_id=10` ledger rows were marked `restore_status=restored`.

This violates MUST RECHECK item 3: final-state folding must not mis-delete/mis-restore other actions, and `rewrite_restored` must preserve unrecovered records. Here it marks an unrecovered user-0 action restored.

Required evidence to pass:

- Add `user_id` to the component final-state action key.
- Add a regression test with two component JSONL records that differ only by `user_id`, proving restore executes both `pm enable --user 0 ...` and `pm enable --user 10 ...`, and both are marked restored only after both commands succeed.
